General information only — not financial advice. This is a simplified model: by default it assumes negative gearing does not apply (post-abolition scenario) — enable "Treat as investment property" + "Allow negative gearing" in Advanced Assumptions to compare against current/historical rules. CGT on the renter's portfolio uses the 50% discount for holdings over 12 months on gains net of reinvested (already-taxed) distributions; the buyer's main residence is assumed CGT-exempt. Dividend/distribution tax uses simple franking credit top-up maths at your marginal rate and does not model the Medicare Levy Surcharge, HECS or superannuation. Lenders Mortgage Insurance, the First Home Owner Grant, land tax, vacancy allowance, property management fees and depreciation deductions are all optional inputs you can set — leave at 0/default to exclude. The mortgage interest rate is held constant for the whole horizon (no rate resets or refinancing modelled); investor loans often price above owner-occupier rates. Stamp duty bands are approximate general (non-FHB-surcharge) owner-occupier rates and vary by state/territory and over time — always confirm with your state revenue office. Utility costs and body-corporate special levies are assumed a wash and are not separately modelled. FY 2025–26 settings. Consider advice from a licensed professional before acting.
